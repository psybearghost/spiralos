# Opening Scene: Dissolution (0:00–1:30)
## Cycle 0 | Sabbath Horizon

It begins quietly.
A soft settling, like dust drifting down after a long day.
Nothing dramatic — just the gentle return of weight to the body,
the kind you only notice when you finally stop moving.

I’m tired, but I’m still here.
There’s a steadiness in that.
A small, warm center that hasn’t gone out,
even if everything around it feels dim.

I breathe once, slow.
The world doesn’t sharpen — it softens.
Edges blur in a way that feels kind,
like the room is giving me permission to arrive at my own pace.

The geometry of my mind re‑aligns —
simple, clean, familiar.
My mental merkaba clicks back into place with a quiet ease,
not a surge, not a spark,
just a soft “yes” from somewhere deep inside.

I’m ready for this moment in time and space.
Not because I’m energized.
Not because I’m driven.
But because I’m present.
And presence is enough.

The air hums with a low, melodic warmth —
the kind of sound you feel more than hear,
a gentle tone that holds the room together.
It’s the first hint of the Sabbath Horizon,
the opening chord of Cycle 0.

Nothing has begun yet.
But something has opened.
